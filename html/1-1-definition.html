Prepositional Phrase

1.1 Definition

A prepositional phrase is a fundamental syntactic unit that functions as a modifier to provide specific contextual details within a sentence. According to Quirk et al. (1985), the structure is formally defined as a preposition followed by a prepositional complement, typically a noun phrase known as the object of the preposition. These phrases may include various internal modifierssuch as articles, adjectives, or possessivesto further specify the object. Linguistically, their primary role is to serve as either adjectival modifiers to describe a noun or as adverbial adjuncts (Huddleston & Pullum, 2002) to clarify the time, place, manner, or reason of an action, thereby enabling the high level of precision and detail required for effective communication in a globalized world.
